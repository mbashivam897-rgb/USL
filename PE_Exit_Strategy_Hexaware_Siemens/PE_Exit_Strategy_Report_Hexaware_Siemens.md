## SECTION 1 — INVESTMENT OVERVIEW

### 1.1 Company Overview
Hexaware Technologies is a global IT and business-process services company headquartered in Mumbai/Navi Mumbai, with **31,000+ employees across ~54 offices in 28 countries**. It positions itself as an **"AI-first, AI-everywhere"** digital and technology services provider, serving clients across Banking, Financial Services & Insurance (BFSI), Healthcare & Insurance, Manufacturing & Consumer, Hi-Tech & Professional Services, and Travel, Transportation & Logistics. [Carlyle press release, Feb 2025]

The company's proprietary platforms — including **RapidX™** (digital/cloud), **Tensai®** (AI/automation) and **Amaze®** (cloud modernisation) — anchor a delivery model that blends consulting, application services, BPS and cloud/infrastructure.

### 1.2 The Private-Equity Story (why this is a textbook PE case)
- **2013:** Baring Private Equity Asia (BPEA) acquired a controlling **~70.8%** stake (deal valued the company at ~₹14,210 cr market cap). [NDTV Profit]
- **Oct 2021:** **Carlyle** acquired Hexaware from BPEA/EQT for **~US$3bn enterprise value** (~₹23,200 cr) through a cross-platform deal (Carlyle Partners + Carlyle Asia Partners). [Moneycontrol, CNBC-TV18]
- **Feb 2025:** Carlyle executed a **₹8,750 cr (~US$1bn) IPO**, structured as a **100% Offer-for-Sale** by CA Magnum Holdings, valuing Hexaware at **~₹43,000 cr (~US$5.0–5.4bn)** at the ₹708 upper band. It listed at ₹745.5 and traded up to ~₹778, reaching a market value of **~US$5.4bn** on debut. This was the **largest IT-services IPO globally in over a decade and the largest sponsor-owned IPO in India.** [Carlyle; Capital Brief; Outlook Business]
- Post-IPO, **Carlyle's stake fell from ~95% to ~74.1%** — i.e., the bulk of the sponsor's value remains *un-monetised* and is the subject of this exit analysis. [CNBC-TV18]

### 1.3 Investment Thesis
A PE sponsor's thesis on Hexaware rests on four pillars:
1. **Above-market growth at scale** — Hexaware roughly **doubled revenue** under PE ownership (CY21 → CY25), outpacing tier-1 Indian IT. [ION Analytics / AVCJ]
2. **Operational value-creation playbook** — client-base broadening, digital/AI repositioning, margin engineering, and bolt-on M&A.
3. **Re-rating optionality** — a scaled, growth-accreting mid-cap can command a premium to peers, supporting multiple expansion at exit.
4. **Liquidity flexibility** — a listed currency enables staggered block-deal exits, sponsor-to-sponsor sales, or strategic sale.

### 1.4 Key Strengths
- Consistent double-digit growth; CY24 revenue **US$1,429m (+13.7%)**, CY25 **US$1,537.4m (+7.6%)**. [PR Newswire; Hexaware IR]
- Differentiated **AI-first** narrative and proprietary platforms.
- Diversified vertical mix with deep BFSI/Healthcare anchors.
- Asset-light, cash-generative, high-ROCE model typical of IT services.
- Strong, deal-tested management team retained through two ownership cycles.

### 1.5 Key Risks
- **AI disruption to the headcount-linked model** — 2026 saw Indian IT valuations compress to ~15–18x P/E (near GFC lows) on fears that GenAI erodes linear, people-based revenue. [Economic Times, 2026]
- **Decelerating growth** — CY25 growth slowed to 7.6% (USD) from 13.7%, and the stock de-rated to **~₹498 (Jun-2026)** from a ~₹778 high. [ET Markets; MarketsMojo]
- **Client/vertical concentration** in BFSI exposes it to financial-services IT-budget cycles.
- **Wage inflation, attrition and INR/USD volatility** pressure margins.
- **Sponsor overhang** — Carlyle's large residual stake is itself a technical headwind on the stock.

### 1.6 Market Positioning
Hexaware sits in the **high-growth mid-cap IT services tier** alongside Coforge, Persistent, Mphasis and LTIMindtree — firms that have structurally **out-grown the tier-1 majors (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, HCLTech)**. In H1 FY26, mid-caps Coforge (+36.8%) and Persistent (+18.1%) led growth; Hexaware grew ~7%. [Livemint, Nov 2025]

### 1.8 Strategic Relevance to PE Investors
Hexaware is the **proof-of-concept for the "buy private IT services → professionalise → re-IPO" playbook in India.** For the incumbent sponsor (Carlyle), the strategic question is *sequencing and route* for the residual stake. For a *new* sponsor, Hexaware represents a potential **public-to-private or anchor secondary** opportunity in a sector trading near trough multiples.

## SECTION 9 — EXIT TIMING ANALYSIS

- **Industry cycle:** Indian IT is mid-down-cycle in 2026 (AI-driven de-rating, near-trough multiples). Historically, trough multiples + recovering growth = poor time to *dump* but good time to *hold/stagger*.
- **Capital markets:** Indian equities remain deep and liquid; block-deal appetite from FIIs/DIIs/SWFs is strong despite IT softness.
- **Rates:** A global rate-easing path into 2026–27 is constructive for risk assets and exit multiples.
- **Growth trajectory:** Hexaware's growth dipped to ~7.6%; a **recovery to double digits would be the catalyst to crystallise the Bull case.**

**Recommended holding period & exit year:** Carlyle should **avoid a forced full exit in 2026** (trough multiple). Optimal path is a **staggered monetisation over CY2026–CY2028**, targeting a **full exit by ~CY2028**, conditioned on growth re-acceleration and a sector re-rating. *Total Carlyle hold ≈ 7 years (2021–2028).*
